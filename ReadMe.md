## How I used this model in my humanoid build

- **Arms were NOT used** in my humanoid design (I only used selected body/shell parts).
- I **scaled the parts up to 450% (4.5×)** to build a large humanoid.

> Note: scaling up increases material usage and weight dramatically (volume grows roughly with the cube of scale), so print settings matter a lot.

---

## Important printing note (to save filament + avoid printer strain)

If you decide to print this at large scale:

- **Do NOT print parts as fully solid.**
- Keep the **inner side hollow** and use a **lightweight structure**:
  - Low infill (e.g., ~5–15% depending on part/strength needs)
  - Reasonable wall/perimeter thickness (enough for rigidity)
  - Consider internal ribs instead of dense infill for big parts

Printing large parts “solid” will:
- Consume a *lot* of filament,
- Make the humanoid unnecessarily heavy,
- Increase print time and heat/load,
- Potentially **stress or damage non-commercial 3D printers** (missed steps, overheating, clogs, bed adhesion failures, etc.).

**You’re responsible for your own printer settings and safety.**
